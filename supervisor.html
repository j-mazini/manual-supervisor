Your Role at a Glance

The Supervisor is the operations lead. You check the week's plan, set up each day, run the operation in the field and follow how it is going. You work side by side with the Administrator on everything operational — but money matters and system setup stay with the Administrator. This manual explains, in plain language, what you do, what you can open, what is off-limits and how a normal week flows.

03

What You Do Day to Day

The Daily Game Plan is where you create, edit and adjust everything the operation needs. The Week Planner you only read, to see what is coming. Here is what that looks like in practice.

In the Daily Game Plan — where you act

This is your working screen. You set up the day and keep it moving as things change in the field.

Set up the day

Create and edit the day's operations from what the week's plan shows.

Handle changes

Deal with absences, swaps, route changes and extra demands in real time.

Move operations along

Mark drivers working or off, and move each operation through its stages.

Finish the day

Mark pending items as finished and close the day when it is done.

The day's history is protected

Once a day is finished it locks, so records already closed cannot be changed by mistake. Give the day a last look before you finish it.

In the Week Planner — view only

You open the seven-day grid to see how drivers, routes and vehicles are arranged for the week, so you can prepare your day. You read it; the week itself is set up by the Administrator.

View only

Move & export

Move operations through their stages during the day, and export the week's plan when you need to share or keep a copy.

Broad operations

You can chat with anyone

You can message Administrators, other Supervisors and drivers. Drivers, on their side, can only message an Administrator or a Supervisor — so you are always reachable for the team.

05

What Is Off-Limits

A few areas are kept for the Administrator only. This is not a restriction on you doing your job — it just keeps each role focused. Here is what you will not see, and why that is normal.

Finance

Invoices, financial reports, deductions and disbursements are not part of your access. The Financial area simply does not show in your menu.

Admin only

System setup

Registering vendors, vehicles, contracts and routes is part of system setup, which is handled by the Administrator. You use what is set up there, but you do not change it.

Master data

Final billing review

The final review screen that prepares operations for billing belongs to the Administrator. Your reports and insights stay fully open to you — that is a different thing.

Admin only

Other people's private data

You only see your own personal entries, never another user's. The operation is shared; private details stay private to each person.

Privacy

If you open the wrong screen

Nothing breaks. If you try to reach an area that is not yours, the system quietly returns you to the Daily Game Plan — your home base.

06

How a Normal Week Flows

Most weeks follow the same simple rhythm: plan, build, run, review. Here is the order you will usually work in.

Your four steps

Check the week. In the Week Planner, see how drivers, routes and vehicles are arranged for the days ahead, so you know what is coming.

Build the day. In the Daily Game Plan, set up the day's operations and get them ready to run.

Run and monitor. Move operations along — from not started, to working or off, to finished — while watching the reports.

Review and handle requests. Check Vendor Performance and clear the Requests Inbox to keep things moving.

Clear yesterday before today

If any of the last seven days is still pending, the system asks you to finish it before moving on. This keeps the records complete and in order.

Safety gate

Finish closes the day

When the day is done, finishing it hands the validated work over to the next stage. After that the day is locked, so always give it a last look before you finish.

Final step

Tips & Common Situations

A few practical points to help the operation run smoothly. Each one is marked by how much it matters, so you know where to be extra careful.

How a day moves — and locks

Every day goes through three stages. Once a day is finished it cannot be reopened — that is the most important thing to remember.

→
In Progressday being operated

Finishedlocked · final

No going back

Once a day is closed you cannot undo it. If something is wrong after that, it has to be corrected another way. Giving each day a final check before finishing keeps the operation reliable.

When you check the week

  • Attention

    If the week looks wrong or incomplete, it is set up by the Administrator — let them know instead of trying to change it yourself.

  • Minor

    Read the week before you build the day, so your Daily Game Plan matches what is planned.

When you run the day

  • Attention

    A pending day in the last seven days will stop you from moving forward — clear it first.

  • Critical

    Bulk actions, like finishing many records at once, cannot be undone — confirm before you run them.

About the team setup

  • Attention

    If a driver has no usual route or vehicle set, they cannot be placed automatically — ask the Administrator to complete their record.

  • Minor

    A driver marked as "Spare" or inactive will not appear in the schedule. That is expected, not a bug.
