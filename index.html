What You Do Day to Day

The Week Planner defines the future schedule; the Daily Game Plan turns it into the real operation. You can create and adjust work in both, subject to future-date, depot-assignment, conflict and status rules.

In the Daily Game Plan — where you act

This is your working screen. You set up the day and keep it moving as things change in the field.

Set up the day

Create and edit the day's operations from what the week's plan shows.

Handle changes

Deal with absences, swaps, route changes and extra demands in real time.

Move operations along

Mark drivers working or off, and move each operation through its stages.

Finish the day

Mark pending items as finished and close the day when it is done.

The day's history is protected

Once a day is finished it locks, so records already closed cannot be changed by mistake. Give the day a last look before you finish it.

In the Week Planner — plan and adjust

Generate the week, edit future allocations, use drag and drop, copy to next week, add Day Off before generation, add Ad-Hoc or Not Allocated records, and finish or export the plan.

Operational planning

Generate with care

Generate Week combines existing active vendors, default routes and vehicles. It can leave a partial week if interrupted, so check what was created before running it again.

Batch operation

Operational chat

Use chat to communicate with the operational team, coordinate schedule changes, clarify incidents and keep day-to-day decisions aligned.

Availability & Access Rules

The Supervisor workspace is centered on operations. These functions do not appear in the available navigation and do not affect the planning, execution and reporting tasks covered by this manual.

Financial management

Invoice management, financial reports, administrative deductions, disbursements and recharges are not part of your access. Your own Deductions and Mobile Invoice views remain available for consultation.

Not available

System setup

The workspace uses existing vendors, vehicles, contracts, routes and operational parameters. Creating or changing those master records is not available from the Supervisor screens.

Master data

Final billing review

The final billing-preparation screen is not available. Operational reports and performance insights remain available for reviewing the work completed.

Not available

Protected personal data

Deductions and similar private entries are limited to your own records and service partner. Operational information remains available according to the selected depot and period.

Privacy

Request decisions

You can create and track requests, but the dedicated Requests Inbox is outside your whitelist. Approving, editing and rejecting requests are not available from your workspace.

Track only

If you open the wrong screen

Nothing breaks. If you try to reach an area that is not yours, the system quietly returns you to the Daily Game Plan — your home base.

How a Normal Week Flows

Most weeks follow the same simple rhythm: plan, build, run, review. Here is the order you will usually work in.

Your four steps

Build the week. Record known Day Off items, run Generate Week, check completeness, then adjust routes, vehicles, reserves and confirmed ad-hocs.

Open the day. In the Daily Game Plan, load the planned work and add substitutions, urgent changes or last-minute ad-hocs.

Run and monitor. Move operations along — from not started, to working or off, to finished — while watching the reports.

Review and follow up. Check operational reports and Vendor Performance, then create or track requests that still need a decision.

Clear yesterday before today

If any of the last seven days is still pending, the system asks you to finish it before moving on. This keeps the records complete and in order.

Safety gate

Finish closes the day

When the day is done, finishing it hands the validated work over to the next stage. After that the day is locked, so always give it a last look before you finish.

Tips & Common Situations

A few practical points to help the operation run smoothly. Each one is marked by how much it matters, so you know where to be extra careful.

How an operation moves — and locks

Status changes follow a fixed state machine. Once an operation is Finished it cannot be reopened — that is the most important thing to remember.

NotAllocatedawaiting a final assignment
→
Workingoperation in progress
→
Finishedlocked · final

A NotAllocated operation may also move to OFF; OFF can return to Working. Finished has no outgoing transition.

Critical · can stall the day or affect records Attention · slows you down or needs care Minor · a small annoyance, no real risk

No going back

Once a day is closed you cannot undo it. If something is wrong after that, it has to be corrected another way. Giving each day a final check before finishing keeps the operation reliable.

When you check the week

  • Attention

    Enter known Day Off records before Generate Week, or the driver may be allocated to the default route.

  • Critical

    If generation is interrupted, inspect the week before retrying so you do not duplicate a partially generated schedule.

When you run the day

  • Attention

    A pending day in the last seven days will stop you from moving forward — clear it first.

  • Critical

    Bulk actions, like finishing many records at once, cannot be undone — confirm before you run them.

About the team setup

  • Attention

    If a driver has no usual route or vehicle set, they cannot be placed automatically. Report the missing setup for correction.

  • Minor

    A driver marked as "Spare" or inactive will not appear in the schedule. That is expected, not a bug.

What you might seeWhat it means
"I can't edit this day"→The day has already passed or is already finished.
"A driver vanished from the schedule"→They may be inactive, missing a route or vehicle, or marked as "Spare".
"The day won't open"→There is a pending day in the last seven days, or no authorized depot leader is assigned.
"Finance doesn't show up"→Expected — finance is not part of your role.
"It sent me back to the Daily Game Plan"→You opened an area that is not yours; it just returns you home.
